## Inventory

**Navigate to:** Dashboard → Inventory

Manages raw materials and ingredients (not finished food products — those are in **Settings → Products**).

### Viewing Stock Levels

The main inventory tab shows all items with current stock on hand, unit of measure, reorder point, and status indicator.

### Adding a Stock Movement

1. Click **Add Movement**
2. Select the item
3. Choose movement type:
   - **Received** — new stock delivered from supplier
   - **Used** — consumed in cooking/production
   - **Wasted** — spoiled or thrown out
   - **Adjustment** — manual correction to match physical count
4. Enter the quantity and a note/reason
5. Click **Save**

### Stock Ledger

Click any inventory item to see its full ledger — a history of every movement with timestamps and the person who recorded it.

### Stock Counts

**Tab: Stock Counts**

Used for physical counts (counting actual stock on hand):

1. Click **New Count**
2. Select the items to count
3. Enter the physical quantity for each item
4. Submit — the system records the variance between expected and actual

### Purchase Orders

**Tab: Purchase Orders**

1. Click **New Purchase Order**
2. Select the supplier
3. Add items and quantities needed
4. Save as **Draft** or set to **Ordered** (sent to supplier)
5. When goods arrive, click **Receive** on the order to update inventory

### Goods Receipts

**Tab: Goods Receipts**

When a purchase order arrives, create a goods receipt to update stock:

1. Open the purchase order and click **Receive**
2. Enter the actual quantities received (may differ from ordered)
3. Note any damaged or short items
4. Click **Confirm Receipt** to update inventory levels
5. The receipt records who received it and the timestamp

### Waste Log

**Tab: Waste Log**

Track spoilage, breakage, and waste:

1. Click **Log Waste**
2. Select the product, quantity, and reason (Expired, Damaged, Overproduction, etc.)
3. The estimated cost is calculated automatically from the item's cost price
4. Edit or delete waste entries if logged incorrectly
5. View waste summary by department to spot patterns

### Recipes & Components

Products with recipes (e.g., a meal deal) have their components listed under **Settings → Products → Components**. When these products are sold, production tracking automatically deducts the component quantities from inventory.

---

### Transfers

**Tab: Transfers**

Move stock between locations:

1. Click **New Transfer**
2. Select: From Location → To Location
3. Add items and quantities
4. Submit

## Stock Alerts

**Navigate to:** Dashboard → Stock Alerts

> You will see a **number badge** on this menu item in the sidebar when there are unacknowledged alerts.

When an inventory item falls to or below its **reorder point**, an alert is automatically generated.

### Viewing & Acknowledging Alerts

Alerts show the item name, current stock level, reorder point, and alert type (Low Stock or Out of Stock).

- Use the filter to toggle between All and Unacknowledged alerts
- Click **Acknowledge** to mark an alert as seen/handled — this removes the sidebar badge

### Auto-Generate Purchase Order

For items with a preferred supplier set, click **Auto PO** to automatically create a purchase order.

## Suppliers

**Navigate to:** Dashboard → Suppliers

The full vendor list from Bettencourt's Vendor Register is pre-loaded (67 suppliers), including Alabama Trading, Ansa McAl, Bank DIH Ltd, Royal Chicken-Mohamed Farms, Namilco, GPL, GT&T, GWI, NIS, GRA, and all others.

### Adding a Supplier

1. Click **New Supplier**
2. Enter: Name, Contact Person, Phone, Email, Address
3. Assign **categories** (Food & Beverage, Utilities, Cleaning Supplies, etc.) — these power the supplier filter cards on the Expenses page
4. Click **Save**

### Editing a Supplier

Click a supplier's name in the list to edit any detail (phone, address, contact person, categories).

### How Suppliers Connect to the Rest of the System

- **Purchase Orders** — select supplier when creating a PO; their details pre-fill
- **Inventory Items** — each item can have a preferred supplier for Auto PO generation
- **Expenses** — top supplier cards on the Expenses page filter entries by supplier
- **Stock Alerts** — Auto PO uses the item's preferred supplier automatically

## Labels

**Navigate to:** Dashboard → Labels

Print price labels or shelf labels for products.

1. Search for or select products to label
2. Choose a label format (price tag, shelf label, etc.)
3. Click **Print Labels**
